# Chichipolies — Product Context

**Register:** product (app UI; design serves the reading and reporting experience).

## What it is

Liberia's community-driven news platform. Citizens across all 15 counties post first-hand stories; the community votes True/False on each one, producing a visible verification status (Unverified → Likely True / Likely False / Disputed). Comments, abuse reports, and an admin moderation panel complete the loop. AI helpers (Claude) polish drafts and suggest comments.

## Users

- **Readers** — Liberians at home and in the diaspora checking what is happening in their county. Mostly on mid-range Android phones, often on slow connections. Guest access to everything readable.
- **Reporters** — registered citizens posting what they witnessed. Not journalists; the submit flow must feel approachable, not bureaucratic.
- **Moderators** — a small admin team reviewing reports, banning abusers, deleting spam.

## Tone

Serious about truth, warm about community. A trusted front page, not a social feed. UK English. Plain, specific copy; no exclamation marks, no "Oops".

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards (cold greys, hero-metric cards).
- Social-media engagement patterns (infinite scroll dopamine, like buttons, streaks).
- AI-slop template look: purple gradients, emoji icons, three-equal-card grids, Inter-everywhere.

## Strategic principles

1. **Verification is the product.** Status badges and the vote meter must always be legible at a glance.
2. **Editorial, not app-y.** It should read like a newspaper made by neighbours: serif headlines, hairline dividers, restrained colour.
3. **Phones first.** Every flow must work one-handed on a 375px screen over 3G. Photos matter; keep payloads lean.
4. **Trust through calm.** No dark patterns, no noise. Moderation actions are quiet and reversible where possible.
